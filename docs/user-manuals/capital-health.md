# Capital Health Model — User manual

**Capital Health** focuses on **withdrawal sustainability** and **capital trajectory** over time: desired income, starting capital, returns, inflation, buffers, and optional **stress** and **optimiser** views. It complements **Income Engineering** and precedes **Capital Stress** in the typical journey.

## Before you start

- **Sign in** with membership that includes this model.
- **Currency** follows your **profile advisory market** where configured.
- **GITEX guided** profiles may be redirected to the **platform** instead of the full dashboard—follow the message if that happens.

## Modes

- **Withdrawal** — target monthly income from the portfolio; model projects depletion or sustainability.
- **Growth / compounding** — target future capital; different headline metrics.

Use the on-screen toggles and fields for **time horizon**, **returns**, **inflation**, **buffer**, and **withdrawal rule** (fixed amount vs percentage), as your layout provides.

## Key outputs

- **Risk tier / structural status** — high-level band from your inputs (labels vary by build).
- **Runway**, **capital survival age**, **income gap**, **charts** — all derived from **your** assumptions.
- **Stress test** or scenario sections — illustrative paths, not predictions of markets.

### The Lion’s Verdict (entitlement-dependent)

- **Paid**: full verdict section with narrative blocks (summary, why, system state, next steps).
- **Trial / limited**: same section shows **System Insight (limited access)**; upgrade path via **See plans**.

## PDF report

- **Download / generate report** produces a **Capital Health** PDF for entitled accounts.
- When Lion is **not** included (trial-class), the PDF may show a **trial edition** note and use **structural** wording instead of full Lion narrative—core diagnosis and charts typically remain.

## Saving

- **Save to server** is generally a **paid** feature; trial users work in-session.

## Tips

- Align **withdrawal rule** and **buffer** with what you actually intend to hold in practice.
- If **Income Engineering** showed tight coverage, use Capital Health to see **multi-year** durability.

## Disclaimer

Educational **only**. Not personal advice. Assumptions drive all numbers; validate with a professional.
